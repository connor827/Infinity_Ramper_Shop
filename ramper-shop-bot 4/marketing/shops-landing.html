Frequently asked

Questions, answered.

Do I need a website?

No. Your Telegram bot is the storefront. Buyers browse your catalogue, add to cart, enter shipping details and check out — all inside chat. If you want a landing page to promote your bot, that's separate from Shops and optional.

What do I need to get started?

A Telegram account, a Polygon-compatible wallet address (MetaMask works), and a few minutes. That's it. You create a bot through Telegram's official @BotFather, paste the token into Shops, connect your wallet, add your products, and go live.

Who actually holds the money?

You do. Payments settle as USDC directly to the Polygon wallet address you provide. We never touch the funds at any point. This is what "non-custodial" means — and it's a deliberate architectural choice, not a marketing line.

How do buyers pay if they don't have crypto?

They don't need any. Buyers pay with whatever they'd normally use — card, Apple Pay, Google Pay, or bank transfer. Our regulated on-ramp partners (MoonPay, Ramp, Stripe, Coinbase) handle the conversion behind the scenes. The buyer's experience is a regular card checkout. You receive USDC.

What about chargebacks?

There aren't any. Once a payment confirms on Polygon, it's cryptographically final. The buyer cannot reverse it. This is a significant advantage if you've ever had Stripe hold funds pending a dispute — but it means you should handle refunds through your own process (which Shops supports directly in the dashboard).

Is there a product limit?

No. You can list as many products as you want, take as many orders as you want, across as many bots as you want. There are no tier restrictions or paywalls on product count, order volume, or customer lists.

Can I use Shops internationally?

Yes. Buyers can pay from 150+ countries through our on-ramp partners. You set prices in your preferred currency (USD, EUR, GBP, CAD, AUD, INR) and settlements always arrive as USDC regardless of where the buyer is.

Do you support digital delivery?

Shops has built-in order fulfilment management for physical goods. Digital delivery (download links, unlock codes) is straightforward to add via merchant notes or a follow-up message in the bot. Automated digital delivery is on the roadmap.

What can't I sell?

Anything illegal in your jurisdiction, obviously. Beyond that, the acceptable use policy mirrors Infinity Ramper's — which is deliberately broader than Stripe or PayPal's. If your business has been declined by traditional gateways and isn't outright illegal, it's likely welcome here.
